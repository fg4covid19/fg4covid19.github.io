Alexander Waibel
Karlsruhe Institute of Technology, Germany, Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh
alexander.waibel@kit.edu
Hazım Kemal Ekenel
Istanbul Technical University, Turkey
ekenel@itu.edu.tr

Hazım Kemal Ekenel is a Professor at the Department of Computer Engineering in Istanbul Technical University (ITU), where he directs the Smart Interaction, Machine Intelligence, and Multimedia Technologies (SiMiT) Lab. He received his Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from the University of Karlsruhe (TH) in 2009. He was awarded with a young investigator grant within the framework of the Excellence Initiative of German Research Foundation (DFG) in 2010, with which he has founded the Facial Image Processing and Analysis Group in Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT). He has coordinated the Benchmarking Facial Image Analysis Technologies (BeFIT) initiative and he was the primary organizer of the ​ BeFIT workshops 2011 and ​2012, that were held in conjunction with the ICCV 2011 and ECCV 2012, respectively. He has co-organized the Affective Computing for Mobile HCI workshop (https://www.ac4mobhci2012.itu.edu.tr/) in 2012 and ​Turkish German Multimodal Interaction Summit (https://www.tgmis.itu.edu.tr/) in 2014. He was local organization chair for the International Conference on Multimodal Interaction (ICMI) in 2014. He has been on the program committee for a number of international conferences and workshops including CVPR, ECCV, ICCV, ACCV, FG, ICPR, ICME, ICB, BTAS, and AVSS. He has over ten years of experience in computer vision and machine learning with a focus on face analysis and human perception. He has published more than 100 peer reviewed publications in prestigious international journals, conferences, and workshops in the related fields, which have received over 3000 citations.

Vitomir Štruc
University of Ljubljana, Slovenia
vitomir.struc@fe.uni-lj.si

Vitomir Štruc is an Associate Professor at the University of Ljubljana, Slovenia. He received his doctoral degree from the Faculty of Electrical Engineering in Ljubljana in 2010, for which he was awarded the 2011 Vodovnik award, and is the recipient of the “2015 young researcher award” of the University of Ljubljana. Vitomir's research interests include problems related to biometrics, computer vision, image processing, pattern recognition and machine learning. He participated in several national and EU funded R&D projects (co-)authored more than 100 research papers for leading international peer reviewed journals and conferences in these and related areas. He served in different capacities on the organizing committees of several top-tier vision conferences, including IEEE Face and Gesture, ICB, WACV and IJCB. Most recently he served as the Program Co-Chair for the 2020 edition of the International Joint Conference on Biometrics (IJCB). Vitomir is a Senior Area Editor for the IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics and Security, a Subject Editor for Elsevier’s Signal Processing and an Associate Editor for Pattern Recognition and IET Biometrics. For his editorial work, Vitomir received the “Outstanding Editorial Board Member Award” 2021 from the IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics and Security. He served as an Area Chair for WACV 2018, 2019, 2020, ICPR 2018, Eusipco 2019 and FG 2020, 2021. Dr. Struc is a senior member of the IEEE, a member of IAPR, EURASIP, he is Slovenia’s national contact point for the EAB and the current president of the Slovenian Pattern Recognition Society, the Slovenian branch of IAPR.

Ferda Ofli
Qatar Computing Research Institute, Qatar
fofli@hbku.edu.qa

Ferda Ofli is a senior scientist in Qatar Computing Research Institute's Social Computing group. Before joining QCRI, he was a postdoctoral researcher in the Teleimmersion Lab working with Prof. Ruzena Bajcsy at the University of California, Berkeley, CA, USA from 2010 to 2014. He received the BSc degrees both in Electrical and Electronics Engineering and Computer Engineering, and the PhD degree in Electrical Engineering from Koc University, Istanbul, Turkey, in 2005 and 2010, respectively. Dr. Ofli pursued his PhD work in the Multimedia, Vision and Graphics Laboratory (MVGL) at Koc University under the supervision of Prof. Engin Erzin, Prof. Yucel Yemez and Prof. Murat Tekalp. Dr. Ofli's research interests cover computer vision, machine learning, and multimedia signal processing. He is a senior member of IEEE and ACM with over 60 publications in top-tier conferences and journals including PAMI, CVPR, ECCV, WACV, TMM, JBHI, and JVCI. He won the Top Reviewer Award in CVPR 2020, the ISCRAM CoRe Paper Award in 2020, the ISCRAM Best Insight Paper and Best Paper Runner-up Awards in 2019, the ISCRAM Best Paper Runner-up Award in 2017, the Elsevier JVCI Best Paper Award in 2015, and the IEEE SIU Best Student Paper Award in 2011. He also received the Graduate Studies Excellence Award in 2010 for outstanding academic achievement at Koc University.

Muhammad Imran
Qatar Computing Research Institute, Qatar
mimran@hbku.edu.qa

Muhammad Imran works as a Research Scientist and Lead of the Crisis Computing team at Qatar Computing Research Institute. His interdisciplinary research focuses on natural language processing, social and crisis computing, applied machine learning, and human-computer interaction. Dr. Imran received his PhD in computer science from the University of Trento in 2013. He then worked as a Postdoctoral researcher at QCRI from 2013-2015. Dr. Imran has published over 80 research papers in top-tier international conferences and journals including ACL, SIGIR, ICDM, ICWSM, and WWW. Four of his papers received the "Best Paper Award" and two "Best Paper Runner-up Award." He has been serving as a co-chair of the Social Media Studies track of the ISCRAM international conference since 2014 and has served as Program Committee for many major conferences and workshops.

Fevziye İrem Eyiokur
Karlsruhe Institute of Technology, Germany
fevziye.yaman@kit.edu

Fevziye İrem Eyiokur is a Ph.D. student at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Germany, where she is a research member at the Interactive Systems Lab (ISL). Her research interests include computer vision, machine learning, and focusing on organic machine learning, generative models, analysis of biometric modalities. She is also a member of the OML project, which is funded by the Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF) of Germany, that focuses on developing machine learning systems and methods that are similar to organic learning of humans.

Alperen Kantarcı
Istanbul Technical University, Turkey
kantarcia@itu.edu.tr

Alperen Kantarcı is a graduate research assistant at the Istanbul Technical University. He is also a researcher at Smart Interaction, Machine Intelligence, and Multimedia Technologies (SiMiT) Lab. His research interests include face recognition, face anti spoofing, signature biometrics, contrastive learning and unsupervised learning.
